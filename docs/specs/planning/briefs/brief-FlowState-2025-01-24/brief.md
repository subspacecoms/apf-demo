# Product Brief: FlowState

## Executive Summary
FlowState is a task management application built for knowledge workers who struggle with context switching and the "shallow work" trap. Unlike traditional list-based managers, FlowState treats time and attention as finite resources, actively helping users carve out space for deep work.

## The Problem
Modern task managers have become dumping grounds for "everything." This creates a "scrolling anxiety" where the volume of tasks prevents the user from starting any. Current tools focus on *what* needs to be done, but ignore *when* and *with what energy level* it should be done.

## The Solution
A task orchestrator that uses "Focus Windows." Tasks are categorized by cognitive demand (Low, Medium, High). FlowState syncs with the user's calendar and suggests specific tasks based on available time blocks and predicted energy levels.

## What Makes This Different
- **Cognitive Load Balancing:** It won't let you schedule 8 hours of "High Energy" tasks in one day.
- **The "Waiting Room":** A low-friction capture zone that hidden from the main view until the user's scheduled "Admin/Shallow Work" block.
- **Anti-Notification Engine:** It suppresses system notifications when a "Deep Work" task is active.

## Who This Serves
- **The "Overwhelmed Expert":** Developers, researchers, and writers who have complex, multi-hour tasks that are constantly interrupted by small "admin" chores.

## Success Criteria
- **Deep Work Ratio:** Users spending 20% more time in uninterrupted focus blocks within 3 months.
- **End-of-Day Satisfaction:** High qualitative scores in daily "How do you feel about your progress?" prompts.

## Scope
- **In:** Calendar sync (Google/Outlook), Task energy tagging, Focus Window scheduling, Basic "Waiting Room" capture.
- **Out:** Team collaboration features, recurring complex sub-tasks, mobile app (V1 is Web/Desktop only).

## Vision
To become an AI-driven cognitive load balancer that automatically negotiates your schedule with external stakeholders to protect your most productive hours.
